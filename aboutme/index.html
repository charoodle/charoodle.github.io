About Me

Page Summary

  • I graduated from University of California, Irvine in 2022.
  • I have an extensive gaming history that spans from 2005 to present day, spanning a variety of game experiences. I was able to learn about all the different types of games there are out there, as well as meet all sorts of people in online communities.
  • I want to use my experiences to help create memorable, unique experiences for future gamers, just as games did for me growing up.
  • I love and respect the types of games that allow people to express themselves and be creative with their game worlds.
  • Let's talk! I'd love to discuss any games mentioned throughout this page! (Favorite genres: FPS, Rhythm)

Education

  • I graduated from University of California, Irvine with a Bachelor's Degree in Computer Game Science in 2022.
  • I joined the UCI Video Game Design Club (VGDC) briefly.
    • Due to scheduling circumstances, I could only join for a quarter to make Touhou-like gameplay for Lead Road to the King. But the experience I gained from working with others that quarter was invaluable, and encouraged me to keep pursuing game development.
  • I attended classes and worked with many amazing professors and peers.

Gaming History

2005 - Introduction to fun, imaginative worlds:

  • I've been gaming since ~2005, when I was first introduced to the Nintendo GameBoy.
    • I was in love with Nintendo games and consoles for their wacky, fresh ideas and gameplay.
      • Mario Party for its ability to let you play games with friends and family.
      • Mario Tennis Power Tour for being able to make an tennis-based RPG adventure with wacky minigames and unique shot abilities.
      • Mario Galaxy with it's gravity-based platforming mechanics.
      • Pokemon for its sense of adventure to reach the top.

2008 - Making friends far and wide in online gaming communities:

  • Animal Crossing: City Folk, Population Growing
    • With Nintendo's Wi-Fi moto: "There's always someone to play!" I was able to find an online forum community where I spent time interacting with community members, managing a tiny virtual shopfront, and eventually meeting a cozy group of friends to enjoy the game with.
  • Roblox
    • I first learned about online communities here, hopping in and out of other player's Places and seeing what kind of games they've built, brick by brick, script by script.
    • I tried my hand at building my own Place, but it wasn't so easy as I thought.
  • Blockland
    • I was enamoured with building-type games.
    • I learned about a basic form of programming using Blockland's Eventing system. A list of commands to make something happen inside the game.
      • Spent my time figuring out how to recreate other game's mechanics, such a DDR machine, a killstreak system from Call of Duty, a search-and-destroy bomb timer also from Call of Duty, and a vault-opening sequence from Payday, or the top-down shooter gameplay of Alien Swarm.
    • I loved the endless form of user-content that Blockland allowed, with both custom maps to play on, and lego-like building capabilities.
  • Minecraft
    • Another building game. I loved the endless world exploration and creativity that Minecraft offered. With friends, the adventure choices were virtually infinite. Each day brought something new with the friend group I played with here.
  • Call of Duty 4: Modern Warfare
    • This was my introduction to first-person-shooter games. I met a group of people here who I played with daily over Xfire. They spent time to show me how to glitch out of bounds on various maps, and of course I got addicted to the usual COD gameplay loop that we all know today. I would continue to play other games later in the series, such as MW2, MW3, BO2, MW2019, and Warzone.
  • DayZ Mod, Arma II / III
    • I have fond memories of playing this survival/sandbox game. Found other survivors through voice chat and they took me in to live in their settlement. They took me on looting runs and heli-raids and we became good friends.
    • I would find online lobbies to play the ARMA III Zeus gamemode, both participating as a player, as well as the Zeus overlord. My game campaigns weren't that creative or interesting, but they did allow me to start creating real-time experiences for other players.
  • osu!
    • A rhythm game that I deeply respect for it's community-driven approach, which helps keep its players tightly-knit even after many years.
    • A community of players who just simply enjoy clicking circles to the beat of songs.
    • One of my most favorite games to this day.

2020 - Chasing my desire to give back to the gaming community

  • I wanted to chase game development more seriously, so I dedicated my studies towards it.
    • I met amazing professors and peers along the way, and all of those experiences supported my choice to chase game development.
  • I kept working on projects as my time as a student, as well as post-graduation, applying game design and programming lessons I've learned directly to my projects.
